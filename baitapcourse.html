Welcom to the Temple of protein.

This place is packed with high-protein foods that will get you jacked or even turn you into a total mass monster.

First up, we have level-1 for those who know absolute nothing about protein.

Now, let me show you what those proteins actually are...And that is plant-based protein

And i'm basing these ratings on a 100g serving.

  • Firm Tofu: 6.8g of protein(1/10).Its actually pretty low, i'm not a big fan of this one.
  • Tempeh: 16.1g protein(3.5/10).Its somewhere in the middle.
  • Seitan: 21.2g protein(5.2/10).Pretty high.
  • Textured Vegetable Protein: 42.5g protein(6/10).

The reason i don't rate plant-based protein highly is that they contain incomplete amino acids; however, it's perfectly fine if you're a vegetarian.

Moving on to the next level: Level-2(Egg & Dairy) - The Convenience Kings

These are the flexible proteins. Great for snacks or adding a boost to your main meals. But if you have a lactose allergy or are a lactose intolerant, i wouldn't recommend this to you.

  • Milk: 3.5g protein(2/10). Basically water with a bit of protein. Don't rely on this.
  • Cottage Cheese: 11g protein(6/10). Great slow-digesting protein (casein) for nighttime.
  • Greek Yogurt: 10g protein(6.5/10). Low calorie-to-protein ratio. Easy to crush a big bowl.
  • Hard Cheese(Parmesan/Cheddar): 25g protein(5/10). Massive protein, but the fat content is a trap. Be careful.

And now...Meet the temple's gatekeepers. They may stand at the entrance, but they pack way more minerals than the ones deep within the shrine: Level-3:Seafood - Lean and Mean from the Ocean

If you want protein without the heavy feeling of red meat, this is your zone. Plus, you get those Omega-3s.

  • Shrimp: 21g protein(6.5/10). Good, but you have to eat a lot of them to hit your goals.
  • Cod/Trout: 22g protein(7/10). Very light, great for cutting phases.
  • Salmon: 25g protein(7.5/10). High protein, but high calorie due to healthy fats. Use wisely.
  • Canned Tuna: 26g protein(9/10). Pure protein. It's cheap, convenient, and incredibly effective.

And here they are: the throne holders, the kings who shall never be overthrown...Level-4: Meat & Poultry - The Heavy Hitters

Moving up from plant-based, we have the animal kingdom. These are complete proteins, meaning they hit all the essential amino acids your muscles crave.

  • Beef (Lean cuts): 24.5g protein (7/10). Solid choice, but the saturated fat keeps it from a higher rank.
  • Bison/Venison: 26g protein (8/10). Leaner and meaner than beef. Excellent for clean bulking.
  • Chicken Breast: 28g protein (8.5/10). The GOAT for a reason. High protein, almost zero fat.
  • Turkey Breast: 30g protein (9/10). Even leaner than chicken. It's the ultimate protein cheat code.

Protein helps you jacked, but other nutrients are just a crucial. If protein is the one on the throne, then carbs, fat, and fiber are the ones who balance the kingdom and help it stable.
